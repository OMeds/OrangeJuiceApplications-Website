## 1. Permitted use

You may use the App only for **lawful** personal or internal business purposes related to managing **your** Apple Contacts and applying profile photos you have the right to use.

Permitted examples:

- Updating colleagues’ photos where your employer permits it and subjects are informed as required;  
- Maintaining accurate client or customer cards in a CRM-linked workflow you control;  
- Saving public social handles you are allowed to associate with a contact;  
- Reviewing ambiguous matches before applying photos.

## 2. Prohibited conduct

You must **not**:

### 2.1 Privacy and rights violations

- Apply photos or identifiers to contacts **without** a valid legal basis under applicable privacy law (GDPR, UK GDPR, CCPA, etc.).  
- Use the App to **stalk, harass, threaten, defame, or discriminate** against any person.  
- **Impersonate** another person or misrepresent affiliation.  
- Process special-category personal data (e.g. health, religion, sexual orientation) through the App unless you have explicit lawful authority and safeguards.

### 2.2 Data misuse

- **Bulk-harvest** third-party profile data beyond normal in-App matching and sync flows.  
- Build or sell **contact lists, datasets, or profiles** derived from the App for unrelated marketing or surveillance.  
- Combine App output with other data to infer sensitive attributes about individuals without lawful basis.

### 2.3 Technical abuse

- **Circumvent** technical access controls, rate limits, OAuth scopes, or security settings (URL policy, on-device processing mode).  
- **Reverse engineer**, decompile, or tamper with the App except where law expressly permits.  
- Introduce **malware**, probe systems, or attempt unauthorized access to our or Apple’s infrastructure.  
- **Overload** third-party APIs or use automated scripts outside the App’s intended UI and shortcuts.

### 2.4 Content violations

- Upload or apply **unlawful, infringing, obscene, or hateful** images.  
- Apply images you know to be **false** or misleading in a context that could harm the data subject.

### 2.5 Platform and Apple rules

- Violate **Apple App Store Review Guidelines**, **Apple Developer Program License Agreement**, or any **connected platform’s** terms (Facebook, LinkedIn, Microsoft, Slack, etc.).  
- Use the App in embargoed jurisdictions or if you are on restricted party lists.
